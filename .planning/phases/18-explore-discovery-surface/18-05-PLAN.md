<objective>
Wire the Server Action invalidation matrix from RESEARCH §Pattern 6. After a user follows or unfollows someone, their Popular Collectors rail must reflect the change on the next render (read-your-own-writes via `updateTag`). After any user adds or removes a watch, the global Trending + Gaining Traction rails must eventually reflect the change (cross-user SWR via `revalidateTag('explore', 'max')`).

This plan depends on Plan 02 because the cache tag strings — `explore:popular-collectors:viewer:${id}` and the bare `explore` fan-out — must exist as `cacheTag(...)` calls in the rail components for the invalidation to take effect.

Purpose: Without these hooks, the rails remain stuck at their cacheLife TTL (5min for Popular/Trending, 24h for Gaining Traction), producing user-visible "I just followed someone but they're still in the rail" or "I just added a watch but Trending doesn't know yet" bugs. The matrix surfaces fresh state at the right granularity.
Output: Two modified action files + two test extensions.
</objective>
