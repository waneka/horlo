## Color

App surface tokens (from `src/app/globals.css` â€” light mode shown; dark mode
auto-handled by the same token names):

| Role | Value | Usage |
|------|-------|-------|
| Dominant (60%) | `--background` `oklch(0.985 0.003 75)` | Settings page background, modal backdrop area |
| Secondary (30%) | `--card` `oklch(1 0 0)` | Danger Zone card surface, modal content surface |
| Accent (10%) | `--accent` `oklch(0.76 0.12 75)` (warm amber/gold) | NOT used in the Danger Zone surface â€” see reserved-for note |
| Destructive | `--destructive` `oklch(0.55 0.22 27)` (red) | Danger Zone framing: card border, section title, trigger buttons, modal execute buttons |

**Accent reserved for:** brand identity elements elsewhere in the app
(active-state highlights, the email wordmark). The Danger Zone surface
deliberately uses ZERO accent â€” destructive flows must read as danger, not
brand. Accent is NOT applied to any element in `WipeCollectionModal`,
`DeleteAccountModal`, or `DangerZoneSection`.

**Destructive treatment â€” load-bearing.** The shadcn `button` `destructive`
variant in this project is a SOFT-TINT style:
`bg-destructive/10 text-destructive hover:bg-destructive/20` â€” a light red
fill with red text, NOT a solid-red button. The Danger Zone uses this existing
variant; do not author a custom solid-red button. Danger framing:

- **Danger Zone card:** `border border-destructive/30` (subtle red-tinted
  border) on a `--card` surface, `rounded-lg p-6`. Distinguishes the section
  from the neutral-bordered Email/Password subsections above without shouting.
- **Section title** ("Danger Zone"): `text-destructive font-semibold text-lg`.
- **Trigger buttons** ("Wipe Collection", "Delete Account"): `variant="destructive"`
  (soft red tint) + leading lucide icon â€” `Trash2` for Wipe, `UserX` (or
  `OctagonAlert`) for Delete.
- **Modal step-2 execute buttons:** `variant="destructive"`, disabled until the
  type-to-confirm keyword matches exactly (D-05).
- **Cancel / Back buttons:** `variant="outline"` (neutral) â€” matches
  `PasswordReauthDialog`'s Cancel button.
- **Modal step-1 warning screen:** the "what gets destroyed" list is rendered
  inside the `DialogContent` with a `text-destructive` heading line and
  `text-foreground` list items prefixed with a lucide `X` or bullet â€” no extra
  background fill needed; the modal context already signals severity.

No new color values are introduced. All four roles map to existing
`globals.css` tokens.

**Email surface color** (separate â€” `oklch()` is unsupported in email clients
per RESEARCH Pitfall 4): the email accent MUST be a hex literal. Documented in
Email Template Contract.
