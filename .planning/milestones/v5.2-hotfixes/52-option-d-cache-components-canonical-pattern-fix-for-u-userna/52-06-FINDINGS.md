## Why no cross-route findings

Three contributing factors:

1. **`unstable_instant` is currently exported on only one route** (`/u/[username]/[tab]/page.tsx`). The validator only runs full structural inspection at shared layout boundaries of routes that opt INTO instant navigation. Routes without an `unstable_instant` export are not subject to the validation.
2. **The shared layout boundary** between `/u/[username]/[tab]` and its sibling segments (`/u/[username]/followers`, `/u/[username]/following`) is `src/app/u/[username]/layout.tsx`, which was refactored to sync + Suspense in Plan 52-04. The validator's "every shared layout boundary" inspection of the `[tab]` route therefore covers the layout's structure and accepts it.
3. **Adjacent followers/following routes** have their own layout chain (`src/app/u/[username]/followers/layout.tsx` if present, or share the parent `/u/[username]/layout.tsx`). They are NOT currently opting into instant navigation, so the validator does not exercise their structure.

The implication for SEED-014 (consumed by Plan 08): the audit's "30 PPR-classified routes" list remains a future expansion surface. When the sweep phase opts ADDITIONAL routes into `unstable_instant`, **at that point** the validator will run on those routes and may surface structural defects. Phase 52's contract was narrow — `/u/[username]/[tab]` only — and that contract is now structurally clean.
