## Area 4 — Similarity sub-weight redistribution math

| Option | Description | Selected |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Proportional rescale ×1.25 | numericTrioCosine 0.40→0.50, eraMatch 0.20→0.25, motifsJaccard 0.20→0.25. Preserves 2:1:1 ratio. Algorithmic per existing anti-pattern comment. | ✓ (recommended) |
| Push the 0.20 into eraMatch | numericTrioCosine 0.40, eraMatch 0.40, motifsJaccard 0.20. Aligns similarity weight with verdict template axis. | |
| Even split across all 3 (+0.067 each) | numericTrioCosine 0.467, eraMatch 0.267, motifsJaccard 0.267. Loses trio-2x-categorical ratio. | |

**Claude's recommendation (and rationale):** Proportional rescale. Preserves architectural intent (Phase 38 D-03's 2:1:1 ratio). No actual signal loss to compensate for: per spike Q1, the archetype-categorical signal is already double-counted today (archetypeMatch 0.04 effective + styleTags 0.20 effective overlap on the same archetype value). Removing the redundant 0.04 channel doesn't lose genre signal — it removes a duplicate channel. Push-to-eraMatch (option 2) would bet the engine's calibration on the verdict-narrative choice — better to keep engine math conservative and let the narrative shift stand on its own.

**User's choice:** Proportional rescale ×1.25.

**Locked decisions:** D-SIM-01 through D-SIM-04 (see CONTEXT.md).
